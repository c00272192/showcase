Business Model

Economic viability and implementation strategies for LiDAR property assessment

Commercial Potential

Beyond academic research, this project demonstrates significant commercial potential for addressing Ireland's housing crisis through technology-enabled property assessment. The business model outlined below presents pathways for sustainable implementation of LiDAR-based assessment at scale.

Implementation Models

Government Partnership

Collaboration with local authorities and national housing agencies to implement LiDAR assessment for public housing stock.

Key Elements:

  • Public-private partnership structure
  • Centralized database of vacant properties
  • Subsidized assessment for affordable housing projects
  • Integration with existing public planning systems
  • Training programs for public sector employees

Commercial Service

Providing property assessment as a service to developers, investors, and property owners interested in redeveloping derelict buildings.

Key Elements:

  • Tiered pricing model based on property size
  • Subscription service for recurring clients
  • Pre-assessment consulting services
  • Value-added reporting and analysis
  • Integration with architectural and planning services

Community Initiative

Enabling community-led housing initiatives through accessible technology and knowledge transfer.

Key Elements:

  • Equipment lending program
  • Community training workshops
  • Open-source assessment tools
  • Crowdsourced vacant property mapping
  • Support for community land trusts

SaaS Platform

Developing a software-as-a-service platform for property professionals to manage their own scanning and assessment.

Key Elements:

  • Cloud-based model processing
  • Automated measurement extraction
  • Reporting and collaboration tools
  • API integration with property management systems
  • Machine learning for condition assessment

Market Analysis

Target Markets

  • Local Authorities: 31 county and city councils managing public housing
  • Housing Agencies: National organizations focused on housing provision
  • Property Developers: Companies specializing in restoration and renovation
  • Architectural Firms: Practices seeking to enhance assessment capabilities
  • Community Housing Groups: Organizations developing community-led housing
  • Individual Property Owners: Owners of vacant or derelict buildings

Market Size

  • 81,000 vacant properties across Ireland
  • €45-89 million potential assessment market using traditional methods
  • €5.6-11.2 million potential annual renovation investment
  • 3,200+ property developers and architectural firms in Ireland
  • 31 local authorities with housing responsibilities
  • 166 registered housing associations and cooperatives

Competitive Landscape

  • Traditional Surveyors: Established but higher cost services
  • Professional LiDAR Services: Higher-end scanning with specialized equipment
  • Digital Property Tools: Software solutions without hardware integration
  • DIY Assessment Tools: Lower quality alternatives with minimal support
  • BIM/CAD Services: Complex solutions requiring technical expertise

Growth Strategy

  • Phase 1: Pilot programs with local authorities (6-12 months)
  • Phase 2: Commercial service launch for property developers (12-18 months)
  • Phase 3: SaaS platform development and release (18-24 months)
  • Phase 4: Community initiatives and knowledge transfer (24-36 months)
  • Phase 5: International expansion to similar markets (36+ months)

Financial Sustainability

Revenue Streams

  • Direct Assessment Services: Fee-for-service scanning and modeling
  • Software Licensing: SaaS platform subscription fees
  • Training Programs: Professional development courses
  • Government Contracts: Public sector implementation agreements
  • Data Analytics: Aggregated property insights and reporting
  • Consulting Services: Strategic implementation guidance

Funding Options

  • Research Grants: Innovation funding from Science Foundation Ireland
  • Housing Agency Partnerships: Co-funded implementation projects
  • Enterprise Ireland Support: Commercialization funding
  • EU Innovation Programs: Horizon Europe funding for housing solutions
  • Private Investment: Angel and venture capital funding
  • University Spinout: Academic commercialization pathway
