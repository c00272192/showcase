Property Assessment of Derelict Buildings in Ireland Using LiDAR and Photogrammetry

Revolutionizing property assessment through advanced technology to address Ireland's housing crisis

View Demo View Full Report

About The Project

Ireland is currently facing a severe housing crisis, with approximately 81,000 abandoned properties scattered across the country. While many of these buildings could be repurposed to address housing shortages, redevelopment remains slow due to a combination of financial, bureaucratic, and logistical barriers.

A key challenge in this process is the uncertainty surrounding initial property evaluations, which can be costly, time-consuming, and often reliant on subjective assessments. Traditional evaluation methods require professional surveyors or architects to conduct manual site inspections, draft sketches, and document structural conditions.

This project investigates whether consumer-grade LiDAR and photogrammetry technologies can provide an efficient, scalable alternative for documenting derelict properties. By using smartphones equipped with LiDAR sensors and photogrammetry applications, this study explores the feasibility of generating accurate, high-fidelity 3D models to support property assessment.

Project Results

Key findings from our research and practical applications

Accuracy Comparison

LiDAR scans achieved 97% accuracy compared to professional measurements, with a margin of error of only ±3cm for most structural elements.

Time Efficiency

Property scanning using smartphone LiDAR required an average of 45 minutes per property, compared to 4-6 hours for traditional assessment methods.

Cost Analysis

Implementation costs were reduced by approximately 78%, with each property assessment costing under €600 compared to the traditional €2,800-€11,000 range.

Stakeholder Feedback

89% of surveyed property developers reported that 3D models provided sufficient information for initial renovation planning and budget estimation.
